Why am I bron?

One of the most common struggles shared by UoN students is not knowing which courses need to be completed, and trying to calculate when they will graduate. This problem is only exacerbated by the incorporation of degree majors, changing timetables, and the balancing act of work, family, and friends. For many students, the path of their degree is not straight and simple. Courses are taken a year early or late, withdrawn from, repeated, failed etc. As current undergraduate students, we have each encountered this problem. Currently students rely on program plans in rigid word document format (reference) which do not allow for changes to the recommended sequence. Although program advisors at best assist with clarifying which courses are remaining to graduate, at worst they can be confusing, slow to respond and unhelpful due to large work loads.

This proposed system will ideally provide students with an online alternative to the existing program plans, incorporating courses that have been completed and credits that have been awarded. The system will also ideally identify the major of student, tailoring their plan to suit. The final system created over the duration of this project will advise students with one or more schedules regarding which courses to study in next semester, as well as the rest of their degree. Not only would the proposed system assist students, but it would also significantly decrease the demand on UoN staff who are currently required to reply to enquiries from its “38,000” students (reference).
